## invite to edit - intro (to be removed from this list)

Hi Synthux community,

I'd like you all to join my effort in building a repository of useful links about anything Synthux related. In a second stage we should start a more thorough archive/gallery showcasing things made and built by the community.

### Future plans

In a second round after collecting the links in this document, I (jonwtr) would also like to start the initiative to make a separate application (website) where the community can submit instruments and hardware mods specifically for Simple instruments, or submit other projects related / that originated within or with help of (feedback) by the community. 

I’m thinking about a gallery type of system similar to how Norns collects their scripts at [norns.community](https://norns.community/), or [Beetlecrab](https://gallery.beetlecrab.audio/patches/) and their gallery for Vector and Tempera. If you are interested in helping out with this, please do get in touch.

I suggest to start 2 repositories on GitHub:
***awesome-synthux-community*** and ***synthux-community-library***
I hope these titles are both clear, descriptive, and convey the unofficial, community-run nature of both projects.

### awesome-synthux-community

### synthux-community-library / synthux-community-hub

"Library" or “Hub” are my personal preferred choices for a gallery-like application, though archive or gallery could be preferred by others? The term library to me evokes a sense of a curated, organized collection of resources (instruments, code, hardware mods) that can be browsed and accessed. Hub sounds more like a central place for all kinds of things and hanging out, and hanging out is what we’re using Discord for?
